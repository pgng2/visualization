Tuition and Admission Information of Undergraduate Programs in the United States

One common question that each prospective undergraduate student always ask themselves is: Which university or universities should a prospective undergraduate student apply for?

The design of this webpage is to help a prospective undergraduate student to explore which universities in the United States that he or she should apply for. The first chart (overview) on the dashboard is made of a scatter plot of SAT equivalent score of admitted students versus in-state tuition fee of universities in the United States. For any prospective undergraduate student, his or her academic ability (SAT equivalent scores) and financial ability (ability to pay the tuition and fees) can be two main driving forces of his/her decision in terms of university application. Having good SAT scores and ability to pay for tuition do not guarantee for admission as admission rate of a university also play an important role. It is common for a prospective student to apply for more than one university (as backup) especially when the targeted university has a relatively low admission rate. Admission rate information is shown on the dashboard overview chart to give a sense of how easy or hard to be admitted into the university.
